Benefits & Concerns of a non-meat diet.

There are many benefits to be eating a non-meat diet. One of the benefits is that is reduces Green-House-Emissions. It is believed that 30% of of all comissions are caused by food production. Meat production is more than two times more polluting than a non-meat diet food production of fruits and veggies. Research predicts that if people were to change to a non-meat diet today, that pollution would decrease by 49%. In addition, non-meat diets save more water than a meat diet. To produce 1kg of plant based food would use 23% less water that 1kg of beef. The meat industry uses 24% of the fress water on this planet. Not to mention that the there the whole earth has about 3% of fresh water.
But along side the benefits, there are concerns to these types of diets. One major issue is that Vegans can be at a greater risk of anaemic (More Info Here). This health condition is caused by the lack of B12 and Omega 3. The effects of the health condition include fatigue and lack of concentration in younger people. It is also believed that there is a link between veganism and lower bone density (Source). Poorly planed vegan diets will cause a lack of calcium, Vitamin D, B2, Iodine, Zinc and so on. Thats why if you want to have a vegan diet, you should very carefully plan it out.
