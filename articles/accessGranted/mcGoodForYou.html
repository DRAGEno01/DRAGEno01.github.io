Minecraft is just a game...

If you were to ask any parent or teacher "Is Minecraft a game? Does Minecraft hold any educational value to it?" most people whould say that it is a game, and it holds no educational value. Yet three schools were studied in Victoria, and each school used Minecraft Education Edition (a special version of the game designed around classrooms and teaching students).

If each school showed improvement, then why do most schools not include the use of the technology.


Steriotypes.

Since Minecraft is concidered to be a game, most schools, and their administrative teams, will not allow Minecraft to be played on school grounds, during or not in the day. This steriotype of 'Minecraft is a game, and thats all that it will be' causes most schools, and most children to miss out of a chance to learn better.


How is it changing lives?

Not only is it making children learn more with more ease, it is also a great game to get children's (or even adults) basic motor functions to develop (Report here). Another benefit is that playing games help increase peoples memory, social skills and problem-solving skills (Report here).


The Future.

If more research is conducted and showed to schools, Minecraft could be a staple part of a childs education. Even in the form of a club, games can help.
