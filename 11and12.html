Year 11 & 12 Information Technology







VCE IT
Year 11 Information Technology is the first step in VCE Information Technology. Year 11 covers the first two units, Units 1 and 2.

UNIT 1
This unit covers how individuals and organizations use ICT (information and communications technology) in their daily lives.
Students acquire and apply a range of knowledge and skills to understand different data types, to create solutions that can be applied to real life.
Students also reflect on how their lives are affected by technology, developing ways to efficiently manage the ways that technology is used in their daily lives.
Learning and examining how networked information systems allow data to be exchanged within a global and local environment,
as well as exploring how mobile devices are used within these networks is also introduced.

UNIT 2
This unit focuses on how individuals and organizations use ICT for a range of purposes. Students apply a range of knowledge and skills to create Android Apps in Java.
Students learn and apply all stages of the problem-solving methodology when creating these solutions. Careers that use ICT will also be investigated.


YEAR 12
Year 12 Information Technology focuses on programming as a strategy for solving problems for a specific audience in a network environment and how ICT can be used to help society.
Students develop a broader knowledge in a programming language which will be used in both Units 3 & 4.

UNIT 3
This unit focuses on programming, a strategy which is used for solving problems aimed towards a specific audience in a networked environment.
Students are expected to have a clear overview of the problem-solving methodology,
which involves students developing and applying knowledge in determining the requirements of solutions,
identifying relevant constraints that should be taken into account when designing the solutions as well as establishing the scope.

UNIT 4
This unit focuses on how the data or information needs of individuals, organizations and society can be met through the creation of designed
solutions within a networked environment while revisiting the four stages of the problem-solving methodology.
