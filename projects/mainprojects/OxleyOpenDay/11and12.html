Year 11 & 12 Information Technology

YEAR 11
In Year 11, students can choose between Computing and Informatics.
Both subjects are semester-long and provide a strong foundation for Year 12.

COMPUTING:
Computing focuses on the development of computer programs to solve problems.
Students will learn programming concepts and techniques using a high-level programming language.
The course covers algorithm development, data structures, and software development methodologies.

INFORMATICS:
Informatics focuses on the effective use of information and information technology within organizations.
Students will learn about data management, networking, and the development of information systems.
The course emphasizes project management and system development life cycles.

YEAR 12
In Year 12, students can continue with either Software Development or Informatics.
Both subjects are year-long and provide pathways to further study in IT-related fields.

SOFTWARE DEVELOPMENT:
Software Development builds on the concepts learned in Year 11 Computing.
Students will develop more complex software solutions and learn advanced programming techniques.
The course includes a major project where students design and implement a software solution.

INFORMATICS:
Informatics in Year 12 builds on the concepts learned in Year 11 Informatics.
Students will develop more complex information systems and learn advanced database and networking concepts.
The course includes a major project where students design and implement an information system.
