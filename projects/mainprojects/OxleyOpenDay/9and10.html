Year 9 & 10 Information Technology

YEAR 9
Information Technology in Year 9 is different from what is taught in Year 7 and Year 8.
The subject splits into different electives: Coding, Robotics and Web Design, allowing students to pursue what interests them most.
The sole purpose of Year 9 Information Technology is to allow students to develop expertise and an in-depth understanding in a range of more specific areas.

ROBOTICS:
Students who choose Robotics will be introduced to computer programming through the use of robots. Students will build Lego Mindstorms robots from plans given to them.
They will then write programs for the robots using the Mindstorms software that will allow the robot to perform various tasks.

CODING:
Steve Jobs, Apples co-founder said, "I think everybody in this country should learn how to program a computer because it teaches you how to think."
This course is intended to introduce students to computer programming and gain a good appreciation of computational thinking and logical thought.
This course provides a strong introduction to the Year 10 IT "Game and App Development", which leads on to Year 11 Computing and Year 12 Software Development.

YEAR 10
Year 10 students can choose one or both of the semester-long IT subjects.
Computer Science Discoveries links to Units 3 and 4 IT Informatics while Game and App Development links to Units 3 and 4 IT Software Development.
Both courses provide a firm foundation for progression on towards VCE Information Technology.

COMPUTER SCIENCE DISCOVERIES:
The course emphasis is on the effective use of information and information technology within an organisation and the development of computer systems for solving problems.
The course incorporates a breadth of knowledge around the technology fundamentals including hardware, databases, networking and interactive web design.
Project management is an integral part of the course.

GAME AND APP DEVELOPMENT:
The increased use of mobile devices and applications is shaping and changing the way we operate and interact with one another.
This course introduces students to game and mobile app development. Students will experience developing simple games.
They will also explore the concepts behind programming and the tools for developing, testing and publishing apps for computers and mobile devices.
